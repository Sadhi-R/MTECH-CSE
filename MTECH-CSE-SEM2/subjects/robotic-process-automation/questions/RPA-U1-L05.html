High WeightageQuestion Bank90%Unit-1Long
Question

Evaluate RPA for enterprise adoption.

DefinitionEvaluating RPA for enterprise adoption assesses process fit, ROI, scalability, security, governance, and change management.

Visual Example

Introduction

Use feasibility matrix: rule-based, stable UI, high volume, low exception rate = good RPA candidate.

Why It Is Needed

Structured evaluation prevents failed pilots and ensures sustainable automation program.

Working Principle

Score processes on volume, repeatability, stability, complexity; calculate ROI; plan CoE (Center of Excellence).

Step-by-Step Explanation

  • Identify candidate processes (rule-based, digital).
  • Score feasibility (volume, stability, exceptions).
  • Estimate ROI: hours saved × cost.
  • Assess IT/security/compliance fit.
  • Plan CoE, governance, and scaling roadmap.

Architecture / Diagram

Process → Feasibility score → ROI calc → Pilot → Scale

Algorithm

Shortlist → PoC → measure KPIs → business case → enterprise rollout

Flowchart

Start -> Define -> Process -> Validate -> Conclude -> End

Real-Time Example

AP invoice processing: 5000/month, 80% straight-through → ROI 12 months, adopt.

Advantages

  • Structured reduces risk
  • ROI-driven prioritization
  • CoE ensures standards

Disadvantages

  • Poor candidates waste investment
  • Underestimating maintenance
  • Ignoring change management

Applications

  • Enterprise automation strategy
  • Shared services optimization
  • Digital transformation

Exam Tips

Give feasibility criteria checklist + ROI formula.

Common Mistakes

  • Automating broken process (fix process first).

Difference Table

Good RPA=rule-based+stable+high volume

Time Complexity

Pilot 4-8 weeks; ROI typically 6-18 months

Space Complexity

CoE team + bot repository
